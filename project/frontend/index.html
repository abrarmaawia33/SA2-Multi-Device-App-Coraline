Coraline (film)

Coraline is a 2009 American gothic stop-motion animated dark fantasy horror film written for the screen and directed by Henry Selick, based on the 2002 novella of the same name by Neil Gaiman.[6] Produced by Laika, as the studio's first feature film,[7] it features the voices of Dakota Fanning, Teri Hatcher, Jennifer Saunders, Dawn French, Keith David, John Hodgman, Robert Bailey Jr., and Ian McShane. The film tells the story of its eponymous character discovering an idealized alternate universe behind a secret door in her new home, unaware that it contains something dark and sinister.

‘Coraline’ Anniversary Re-Release A Smash Hit, Passes $20 Million Worldwide

The top animated film at the U.S. box office last weekend wasn’t an expected cg blockbuster like Despicable Me 4 or Inside Out 2. Rather, it was the re-release of 15-year-old stop-motion film Coraline. The Fathom Events anniversary release of the Henry Selick-directed Laika film opened in fifth place with a smashing weekend haul of $8.3 million (estimated). Since launching last Thursday, the film has generated over $12.5 million in domestic box office, leading Fathom to extend the film’s run through August 29. Coraline is now the highest-grossing classic title release in Fathom’s history. The re-release also outperformed the opening of Laika’s last original film, Missing Link, which launched with $5.9m in 2019.

One of the most detailed and visually stunning stop-motion films ever created, Coraline is famous for its over-the-top animation. With a $60 million budget, Coraline features 150 sets divided in a 140,000-square-foot warehouse. Most of the elements seen in Coraline are handmade, and the creators could only tape around 100 seconds each week of production. Coraline is a timeless stop-motion production highlighting Neil Gaiman's most interesting novella. The film was created with such care and attention to detail, that it takes viewers many watches to unveil everything Coraline has to offer. From little Easter eggs to symbolic appearances to outstanding trivia about its production, Coraline is a fascinating movie from beginning to end.
